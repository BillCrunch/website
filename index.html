The subscription trap

The business of
being forgotten.

Everything nowadays is renting. Sorry, I meant subscription. The economics of subscription is built on forgetting, and the model runs on one resource: your attention, and how little of it you have left over. That gets measured, A/B tested, and refined into four quiet tactics.

Tactic 01

Built to be forgotten

Interfaces are designed to be glanced at once and never opened again.

Tactic 02

Timed for distraction

Charges are scheduled to land while you're thinking about something else.

Tactic 03

Small enough to ignore

Each line looks insignificant on its own. Together, they quietly add up.

Tactic 04

Easy in, hard out

One click to subscribe. Nine pages and a phone call to cancel.

The less you remember, the more they collect. It's not personal, just business.

Subscriptions are designed to be forgotten. BillCrunch is designed to remember.

Privacy first

Your data stays on your phone.
Full stop.

This is not a tagline. BillCrunch has no servers to talk to.

No account

Nothing to sign up for. No email, no password, no identity. Install and use immediately.

No internet required

The app works fully offline. Everything is stored in a local SQLite database on your device. No network calls, ever.

No tracking

No analytics, no ad SDKs, no telemetry. The app cannot see what you do with it, and we do not want to.

You own the backup

Export a full JSON backup to any storage you choose. Restore it anytime. Factory reset wipes everything locally, instantly.

Questions

Good to know.

Is BillCrunch free?

Yes. It is free to download and use, with no account to create and nothing to subscribe to.

Is my data really private?

Yes. Your bills are stored only in a local database on your device. There is no login, no cloud sync, and nothing is sent to a server. This website uses privacy-friendly, cookieless analytics and nothing more.

Does it work without an internet connection?

Completely. BillCrunch does not need a connection to work, because everything runs on your device.

Is there an iPhone version?

Android is available now. An iOS version is in development, and the App Store badge will go live when it ships.

Which currencies are supported?

Fifteen, including AUD, USD, EUR and GBP. You pick one in Settings and every amount shows with that symbol.

Can I move my data to a new phone?

Yes. Export a backup file from Settings, then import it on the new device. You can also export your history as a CSV.
