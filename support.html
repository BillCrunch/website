Backup & Restore

Creating a backup

BillCrunch stores all data locally on your device. To move your data to a new device, or keep a safe copy, use the built-in JSON backup:

  1. Open BillCrunch and tap the Settings icon.
  2. Tap Backup & Restore.
  3. Tap Export backup. A file picker will appear.
  4. Choose where to save the .json file. Your device's Downloads folder or a local folder are good choices.
The backup file contains all your bills, payment history, categories, and settings in plain JSON. Keep it somewhere safe. BillCrunch has no copy of it.

Restoring a backup

  1. Install BillCrunch on the new device (or after a factory reset).
  2. Open the app, go to Settings, then Backup & Restore.
  3. Tap Restore from backup and select your .json file.
  4. Confirm the restore. The app will replace all current data with the backup contents.

CSV export

To export your bill or payment data for use in a spreadsheet:

  1. Go to Settings and tap Export CSV.
  2. Choose a destination and save the file.

The CSV file can be opened in any spreadsheet application such as Google Sheets, Excel, or Numbers.

Factory reset

To wipe all data from the app, go to Settings and tap Factory reset. This permanently deletes everything stored by BillCrunch on your device. Make a backup first if you want to keep your data.

Reminders

Setting up notifications

BillCrunch uses local notifications, generated on your device. No push notification service or external server is involved.

  1. When adding or editing a bill, toggle Reminders on.
  2. Enter one or more lead days. For example, entering 7 and 1 will notify you 7 days before and 1 day before the due date.
  3. Grant notification permission if prompted. You can also grant it from your device's app settings.

Confirm payment from a notification

Tap the notification to open the Confirm Payment screen directly. Log the amount paid. If it differs from the expected amount, you can choose to update the next bill cycle or keep the original amount.

Notifications not appearing

  • Check that notification permission is enabled for BillCrunch in your device's Settings, then Apps, then BillCrunch, then Notifications.
  • Ensure the due date on the bill is in the future.
  • If you recently restored a backup, re-enable reminders on each bill to reschedule them.

Currencies

BillCrunch supports 15 currency symbols. Select a currency per bill. No conversion is performed; amounts are displayed as-is with the chosen symbol.

  • USD ($), GBP (£), EUR (€), AUD (A$), CAD (C$)
  • JPY (¥), CHF (Fr), INR (₹), NZD (NZ$), SGD (S$)
  • HKD (HK$), KRW (₩), BRL (R$), MXN (MX$), ZAR (R)

To change the currency on an existing bill, tap the bill to open its detail view, then tap Edit.

Frequently asked questions

How do I add a bill with an irregular cadence?

When adding a bill, choose Custom from the cadence options. Enter the number of units and select Days, Weeks, or Months. For example, "every 5 weeks" or "every 3 months".

Can I add sub-categories?

Yes. Under each of the six main categories (Property, Subscriptions, Utilities, Insurance, Vehicle, Personal) you can add, rename, or remove sub-categories. Go to Settings, then Categories to manage them.

What is a tag and how is it different from a category?

Categories are the primary grouping (the six defaults). Tags are optional free-form labels you can add to any bill as a secondary filter. For example, you might tag several bills as "Business" to view them separately from personal expenses.

My payment was different from expected. What should I enter?

In the Confirm Payment screen, enter the amount you actually paid. BillCrunch will record the variance. You can then choose whether to update the expected amount for future cycles or leave it as-is.

Can I use the calendar view to see upcoming bills?

Yes. The Calendar tab shows all due dates across the month. Tap a date to see which bills are due on that day.

How do I mark a one-off bill as paid without creating a next cycle?

When you confirm payment for a one-off bill, BillCrunch records it in history and marks it as complete. No next cycle is created. The bill will appear in your history but will no longer appear in the dashboard or forecast.

Is there a dark mode?

BillCrunch follows your device's system appearance setting. If your device is set to dark mode, the app will display in dark mode automatically.
